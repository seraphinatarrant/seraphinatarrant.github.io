About me

I'm Seraphina Goldfarb-Tarrant, and I'm a graduate student in Natural Language Processing (NLP) imminently finishing my MS at the University of Washington.

I do research in Creative Neural Natural Language Generation (a mouthful, aka NLG for short), at the Information Sciences Institute in the lab of Nanyun (Violet) Peng.
I also do Human-Computer Interaction, and have an interest in Multilingual NLP and in Fairness and Bias in Machine Learning.

I have a varied background: I have lived in many places around the world and worked in a few different fields. My homes have been Tokyo, NYC, California, Seattle, Shanghai - and in those places I've worked both for Google and for smaller projects.

As well as being extremely passionate about NLP, I am a professional sailor and spent a few years sailing the North Sea. I also study Rinzai Zen, which is the type with the riddles (koans) and samurai associations.
