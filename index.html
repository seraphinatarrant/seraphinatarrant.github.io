About me

{% include image.html max-width="250px" align="align-left" file="images/seraphina_kangaroo_valley.jpeg" padding="10px" alt="photo of Seraphina" caption=" (^ this is me!)" %}

I'm Seraphina Goldfarb-Tarrant, and I'm a graduate student in Natural Language Processing (NLP) imminently finishing my MS at the University of Washington.

I do research in Creative Neural Natural Language Generation (a mouthful, aka NLG for short), at the Information Sciences Institute in the lab of Nanyun (Violet) Peng.
I also do Human-Computer Interaction, and have an interest in Multilingual NLP and in Fairness and Bias in Machine Learning.

I have a varied background: I have lived in many places around the world and worked in a few different fields. As well as being extremely passionate about NLP, I am a professional sailor and spent a few years sailing the North Sea. I also study Rinzai Zen, which is the type with the riddles (koans) and samurai associations.

Feel free to check out my CV and my publications and (eventually/soon!) my blog.
